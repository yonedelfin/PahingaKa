What is Poetry?

American poet, Lucille Clifton, defines poetry as “a matter of life, not just a matter of language.” In its purest form, it is a form of literary art that uses rhyme schemes and follows a specific rhythm to convey emotions and ideas. From the earliest periods of human history, poetry has allowed us to express ourselves and explore the human condition through the art of writing.
(Definition by Explication)

 What is Filmmaking?

Filmmaking is the process of producing a motion picture. It is the art of creating a reality in which the audience can fully immerse themselves. Only through filmmaking can we experience a multitude of emotions as films enable us to channel our creativity and imagination to the fullest.
(Definition by Definition)

 What is Painting?

Painting is a visual language that allows us to build a whole new universe with just a few simple tools. Simply described, it involves the application of paint and colors to a surface. On a two-dimensional surface, it captures our feelings and thoughts.
(Definition by Clarification)
